Lover of knowledge. Creator of things.

I am a designer who, propelled by curiosity, has been involved with diverse areas of knowledge. I have a background in communications and journalism, policy-making, and languages. I believe in the power of visual story-telling to connect people and spark conversations, and am primarily interested in information design and in creating identity systems.

WORK

Music Website

Previous Next

Web-based platform that compiles contemporary female artists who incorporate their traditional Latin American roots to their music. The pattern for each artist is inspired by traditional textiles of her country, and the colors correspond to the bright pallets found in Latin American graphics.

Art Catalogue

Previous Next

Gertrud Goldschmidt was an artist and a conceptualizer. GEGO is structured around her ideas, and designed by inspiration of her language of space and line. This perfect-bound book is 110 pages long, focused on imagery, and includes Guy Brett's essay "Gego's Forcefields" and risographed papers.

Music Tour Campaing

Previous Next

Fictional music tour campaign inspired by Jorge Drexler’s song “Luna de Rasqui.” It includes an album cover, poster, website, apparel and social media application.

Mobile App

Previous Next

Speculative app that allows users to get instant information on public art and infrastructures by taking a picture while on-the-go. Users can also save their pictures with the information, create albums, and track their route.

Street Art Book

Previous Next

Flip book with personal photographs of stickers found along Macdougal Street in New York City, and personal stickers designed by myself. The book follows an urban aesthetic and includes stickers in the front and back.

Open Typeface

Previous Next

Open typeface designed in FontLab that was inspired by Herb Lubalin's famous ad: "let's talk type." The typography in the ad was handlettered, so the challenge was to bring it to the digital world and develop the remaining 240 characters.

Foodtruck Branding

Previous Next

Speculative re-branding for existing Venezuelan foodtruck Metro Arepas. The system includes the truck letterhead and menu, and is inspired by the friendliness and sense of community behind the business.

Exhibition Brochure

Previous Next

Artist Manolo Valdes and Nobel-prize winner Mario Vargas Llosa collaborated to create The Ladies of Barajas, which stand in Madrid's international airport. This is a brochure for a fictional exhibition on the process of such collaboration. It is inspired by Valdes' original sketches and traditional book aesthetics.

Infographics

Previous Next

Infographics developed for NGO SOS Venezuela NY recounting the crisis that erupted into ongoing manifestations against the government of Venezuela on April 2017. Information includes timeline of events, entities with most conflict and victims detained or killed by government forces.
